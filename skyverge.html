Step-by-Step

We’d like to get an idea of how you explain concepts and processes. What was the last thing your purchased online? Tell us, step-by-step, how you went about searching, choosing, and completing your purchase.

Recently I decided to buy a low-budget mechanical keyboard. I'm subscribed to an online enthusiast community called Massdrop that always promotes mechanical keyboards, so I decided that I would take the plunge and get a started mech keyboard.

The mechanical keyboard community is very passionate, so it is easy to get caught up in the many different arguments about switches, lighting and so on..

I decided I would scour the web for deals on Mechanical Keyboards and from there base my decision on reviews of the keyboard from the community. Most high ratings are derived from the type of switches the keyboard uses. From Cherry switches to Blues or Browns, there are many variations. I wanted keys that were heavier and produced the "clicky" sound. Most importantly, I wanted a keyboard that was minimalistic and not flashy.

After compiling a list of deals on Slickdeals, I wanted to find a keyboard within the $30-50 range, that had an average of 3.5/5 stars and was generally recommended by the community. I went to Youtube, searched for the keyboards I had my eye on and listened to the sound the keyboards made. Next I listened to the reviewer to see what they thought about the ergonomic feel of the keyboard: is it comfortable, is the distance natural, does it fatigue the reviewer easily, etc..

Finally, after watching a several videos on the different keyboards and ghosting, I came to a decision on my first budget keyboard. I settled on a Full Size Blue Switch Mech Keyboard from Monoprice.

Unfortunately, now that I have this keyboard, I'm already looking at more expensive and quality keyboards. I can see why so many people are passionate about them!
